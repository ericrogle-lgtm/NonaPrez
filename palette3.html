For People Building a Life Here

You can feel crowded and still feel alone.

Most people who find us moved to Lake Nona for work. The calendar fills up fast, the kids are little, family is far away, and faith — somewhere in the move — quietly slipped down the list. None of that makes you a stranger here. It makes you exactly who we're glad to meet.

The Calendar

Demanding careers, long commutes, and Sundays that already feel spoken for.

The Distance

A new city, few roots, and the quiet work of starting friendships from scratch.

The Drift

Faith you never abandoned, but haven't had the margin to return to.

“Is there more to life than keeping up?”

How We Come Alongside You

We're not here to perform. We're here to walk with you.

You're not a project to fix or a number to add. You're a person worth knowing. Our job is to walk with you toward something solid.

We're a congregation of the Presbyterian Church in America (PCA) — which simply means we hold to the historic, biblical Christian faith and are cared for by a team of pastors and elders, not run by one personality. In a culture built on performance, we offer rootedness.

Preaching With Depth

Sermons grounded in Scripture — clear for newcomers, nourishing for those who've walked with God for years.

Worship That Anchors

A historic rhythm of prayer, confession, and Scripture that has steadied Christians for centuries.

Family of All Ages

An intergenerational, diverse congregation where children are formed and parents are supported, not stretched.

Pastors You Can Reach

Relational warmth and real access. You won't get lost in a crowd or handed off to a screen.

A Simple Way Forward

You don't have to figure out the next step alone.

No pressure to belong before you're ready. Just a clear path, one step at a time.

Step 01

Visit

Come as you are and experience steady, Scripture-centered worship in a welcoming room.

Step 02

Connect

Move from face-in-the-crowd to known by name through a simple next conversation.

Step 03

Grow

Put down roots in a Life Group, regular worship, and teaching you can actually live on.

Step 04

Serve

Find your place giving back — inside the church and out into the Lake Nona community.

The goal isn't to assimilate you quickly. It's to walk with you faithfully, over time.

Your First Sunday

Here's exactly what to expect.

New to church, or just new to this one? Walking in shouldn't feel like a test. Here's the honest rundown.

Where & When

We gather Sundays at 10:30 AM at the Pioneers Fletcher Center in Lake Nona. Arrive a few minutes early and someone will help you find your way.

What Worship Is Like

We follow a historic order of worship — singing, prayer, Scripture, and a sermon. If a word or moment is new to you, that's expected. Nothing here requires insider knowledge.

What to Wear

Whatever you're comfortable in. You'll see jeans and you'll see jackets. Come as you are — we mean it.

Your Kids Are Welcome

Children's ministry runs during the service for little ones, led by background-checked volunteers who genuinely love kids. Prefer to keep your child with you? That's welcome too.

Will Anyone Bother Me?

You'll be warmly welcomed, never put on the spot. No standing up, no spotlight, no pressure to give. Visit as long as you like before anyone asks anything of you.

After the Service

People stay and talk. Grab coffee, ask questions, meet a pastor. Or slip out quietly — both are completely okay.
